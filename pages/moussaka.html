Moussaka


Description:

Moussaka is a classic Greek dish made with layers of eggplant, minced meat, and a rich béchamel sauce. It is a hearty and flavorful casserole that is often enjoyed as a main course. The dish is baked to perfection, resulting in a golden, creamy top and a deliciously savory filling. Moussaka is a beloved comfort food in Greek cuisine, perfect for family gatherings and special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • eggplants, peeled and cut lengthwise into 1/2 inch thick slices
  • alt to taste
  • cup olive oil
    • 1
  • tablespoon butter
  • pound lean ground beef
  • onions, chopped
  • clove garlic, minced
  • round black pepper to taste
  • tablespoons dried parsley
  • teaspoon fines herbs
  • teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • teaspoon ground nutmeg, divided
  • (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • cup red wine
  • egg, beaten
  • cups milk
  • cup butter
  • tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • round white pepper, to taste
  • ½ cups freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Directions:

Step 1:

  • Lay eggplant slices on paper towels; sprinkle lightly with salt
  • Let sit for 30 minutes to draw out moisture, then pat dry with paper towels

Step 2:

  • Warm olive oil in a skillet over high heat
  • Fry eggplant until browned, 2 to 3 minutes per side
  • Drain on paper towels; set aside

Step 3:

  • Melt 1 tablespoon butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Stir in ground beef, onions, and garlic; season with salt and black pepper. Cook and stir until beef is browned, 8 to 10 minutes.

Step 4:

  • Add parsley, fines herbs, cinnamon, and 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg. Pour in tomato sauce and wine; mix well. Simmer for 20 minutes. Allow to cool, then stir in beaten egg.

Step 5:

  • Scald milk in a saucepan over medium heat.

Step 6:

  • At the same time, melt 1/2 cup butter in a large skillet over medium heat.

Step 7:

  • Whisk flour into butter until smooth. Lower heat; gradually pour in scalded milk, whisking constantly until it thickens. Season béchamel sauce with salt and white pepper.

Step 8:

  • Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

Step 9:

  • Arrange a single layer of eggplant in the prepared baking dish.

Step 10:

  • Cover eggplant with meat sauce, then sprinkle 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese on top. Cover with remaining eggplant and sprinkle another 1/2 cup cheese on top.

Step 11:

  • Pour béchamel sauce over the top and sprinkle with the remaining 1/2 cup cheese and 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg.

Step 12:

  • Bake in the preheated oven until top is golden and bubbly, about 1 hour. Allow to rest for 20 minutes before slicing and serving.

Step 13:

  • Serve and enjoy!
