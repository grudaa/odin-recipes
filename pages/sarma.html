Homemade Sarma


Description:

Sarma is a traditional dish made of cabbage leaves stuffed with a flavorful mixture of minced meat, rice, and spices.
It is commonly enjoyed in many Eastern European and Middle Eastern countries, often served with a dollop of sour cream or yogurt.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head cabbage, frozen for 3 days, then thawed overnight in the refrigerator
  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • ½ pound ground pork
  • ½ pound ground ham
  • 1 cup uncooked long-grain white rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon coarse ground black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 pound sauerkraut
  • 1 cup tomato juice
  • 1 cup water, or as needed to cover

Directions:

Step 1:

  1. Remove the leaves from the head of cabbage

Step 2:

  1. Mix beef, pork, ham, uncooked rice, onion, egg, salt, pepper, and garlic powder together in a bowl until well combined
  2. Form mixture into oblong balls, using 1/2 cup of the mixture at a time Wrap a cabbage leaf around each ball

Step 3:

  1. Spread sauerkraut in the bottom of a large pot
  2. Layer cabbage rolls on top, placing them seam-side down
  3. Pour tomato juice over rolls, then add enough water to cover and bring to a boil
  4. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 3 hours, adding more water as necessary
